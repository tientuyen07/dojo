Fundamentals, A Huge Shinigami

💡 The first approach to any financial instrument, especially with crypto, is very simple: the average user knows absolutely nothing about finance in general or the technical aspects related to it, but he can read and listen very well.

And it is precisely for this reason, driven by the unwavering idea of becoming a millionaire in just a few days, he starts to "study" all those components related to the fundamentals, factors that, in his mind, qualify him as a top-tier investor and someone who "knows."

"Wow, that project is really doing well, look at how the team is pushing: collaborations, platform improvements, DeFi, AI, Gaming... it’s absolutely the future. I’m incredibly bullish; it’s time to accumulate heavily and just wait for the team to do the work... it will make me rich."

How many of you beginners started like this? Here’s one, here’s another... and it's no surprise, I started like this too. The journey then takes an even more visceral turn, as one enters channels forming a community that often takes on a "cult-like" form rather than being a group that develops critical and constructive thinking aimed at investing but also selling when necessary, not just buying and holding. Most of these members at some point develop some sort of a deep love for the project, comparable to the love that a father feels for his son where he's willing to defend him at all costs, even to that of dying. Chronicles of a fallen love, precisely.

And this brings us to the next step: have you ever asked yourself why 90% of the people in this field rely on fundamentals? Because they are easily digestible, do not require extensive study, and can be used as "motivation" to make people believe what the SM want them to.

The price goes up? → It's because the project is performing exceptionally well.

The price goes down? → It's because the team took the money and is smoking it on a beach in Negril.

How is it that there are projects that have been operating for years but their tokens are suffering tremendously and, in many cases, are trending towards zero? How is it that there are projects that were born yesterday, which are complete scams, yet they shoot up by 200-300% in just a few sessions? Because everything is based on speculation, and what many here call "utility" is purely an illusion, a trick designed to attract liquidity from the sheep, no matter what the project is.

The so called "KOLs" are individuals of low moral value who sell their souls to projects in exchange for money, scamming thousands of people. Often, they try to "redeem" themselves by doing charity work... charity that comes from "blood-stained" money.

The most influential pages in the scene are the first to talk about phantom catalysts, like institutions accepting payments in crypto, large companies starting to implement crypto projects, or prominent personalities promoting Web3 projects... but once again, ask yourself why. They are all lies, illusions created specifically to increase FOMO and to make people become exit liquidity, because sooner or later, those at the top will bring the whole thing crashing down.

And if you think these are all conspiracy theories and nothing real, ask yourself if it's really possible for everyone to profit in a highly speculative market while only a few lose. Ask yourself whether the world in general is corrupt or not. If pharmaceutical companies cured you, they'd go bankrupt. If banks helped you get out of debt, they'd collapse. If fundamentals actually provided the necessary information to make successful investments, everyone would be rich. Truth is they just provide "comprehensible justifications" for price movements which are often released at specific HTF levels in order to make sure that sheep take a direction or another.

“But bro, do you have a double personality problem? First, you create a section on how to evaluate fundamentals, and then you tell me they're useless.”

Being able to recognize if a project is legit or a scam and has the “legs” to run is a good addition in your cultural baggage ; and it can help you to understand if it’s wise or not to allocate a certain % during the initial stages. But in this space fundamentals matter 10% while 90% is constituted by technical analysis and price action. Projects work, there are real people behind them and some of them have also good intentions but... this world is dominated by wolves, sharks, entities that want to make money and are here exclusively for that reason (as you are, if you think about it). Will it change? Maybe in the future, but not at the moment.

Your eyes will be fully opened when, to assess an investment, you'll spend more time looking at the chart instead of the fundamentals.

These are facts, undeniable truths as charts are a representation of human's emotions. Whenever you see someone pushing fundamentals when the price is parabolic, ask yourself why. Whenever you see someone pushing fundamentals when the price is going down only, ask yourself why. Whenever you think that an asset will never go down because of its fundamentals, ask yourself who's gonna profit. Whenever you want to buy an asset, ask yourself if the R/R opportunity is favourable and you're not getting sucked into fancy narratives. That's the playbook if you want to survive and thrive. Otherwise you can continue to live in the land of illusions, but don't expect to grow. It's up to you to take the right path.
